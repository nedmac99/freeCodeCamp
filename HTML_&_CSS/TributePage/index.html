Odysseus Tribute Page

The Heroic events of Odysseus

  • (c. 1194–1184 BCE) The Trojan War: Odysseus participates in the decade-long Trojan War, which was triggered by the abduction of Helen
  • (c. 1184 BCE) The Wooden Horse: Odysseus devises the Trojan Horse strategy, leading to the fall of Troy.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) Departure from Troy: Odysseus and his men leave Troy to return to Ithaca.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) The Lotus-Eaters: Encounter with the Lotus-Eaters occurs during the return journey from Troy.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) Cyclops Polyphemus: Odysseus and his crew encounter the Cyclops Polyphemus.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) Aeolus, the Wind King: They visit the island of Aeolus on their way home.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) Circe's Island: Odysseus and his men are lured to the island of Circe.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) The Sirens: Encounter with the Sirens takes place during the voyage.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) Scylla and Charybdis: The ship navigates the perilous waters between Scylla and Charybdis.
  • (c. 1184 BCE) The Cattle of Helios: His men's transgression with Helios' cattle occurs on the island of Thrinacia.
  • (c. 1184–1160 BCE) Calypso's Island: Odysseus is stranded on Calypso's island for several years.
  • (c. 1160 BCE) Return to Ithaca: Odysseus finally returns to Ithaca.
  • (c. 1160 BCE) The Killing of the Suitors: Odysseus, disguised as a beggar, takes back his palace from the suitors.
  • (c. 1160 BCE) Reunion with Penelope: Odysseus is reunited with his wife, Penelope.

"Even his griefs are a joy long after to one that remembers all that he wrought and endured."
